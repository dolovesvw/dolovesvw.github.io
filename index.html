RUSSIAN TRADITION

MASLENITSA

Russia's Traditional Pancake Week
An Eastern Slavic folk-religious festival celebrated during the last week before Orthodox Lent, marking the end of winter and the coming of spring with ancient pagan roots.
History
  • 2nd C. Origins as pagan spring equinox celebration
  • Medieval Incorporated into Orthodox Christian calendar
  • 1917 Survived Russian Revolution, adapted under Soviet rule
  • 2002 Moscow begins annual Red Square festival
  • Today Celebrated globally by Russian diaspora

Cultural Significance
Originally a pagan spring equinox celebration to encourage the sun's return, Maslenitsa was later incorporated into the Orthodox Christian calendar as the final week of feasting before Lent begins. The festival represents the "border between winter and spring" and combines ancient Slavic traditions with Christian customs.

Global Reach
Russian immigrant communities worldwide celebrate Maslenitsa, from the United States to Europe and beyond. The festival is often compared to Western Carnival or Mardi Gras due to its festive atmosphere and timing before Lent. Modern celebrations blend traditional elements with contemporary community events.

Historical Evolution

Pre-988 CE

Pagan Origins: Ancient Slavic spring equinox celebration honoring Yarilo (sun god) and fertility rituals for agricultural success.

10th-16th Century

Christian Adaptation: Orthodox Church integrates pagan festival as Cheese Week before Lent, transforming meanings while preserving traditions.

17th-19th Century

Imperial Codification: Peter the Great and successors formalize Maslenitsa as state celebration, spreading unified customs across the Russian Empire.

1917-1991

Soviet Transformation: Communist regime removes religious elements while maintaining secular folk festival aspects for cultural continuity.

1991-Present

Post-Soviet Revival: Full restoration of religious and cultural significance, now celebrated as UNESCO-recognized cultural heritage.
